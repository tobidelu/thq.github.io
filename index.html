🛡️ SCUML Compliance

Stay Compliant with SCUML Registration

The Special Control Unit Against Money Laundering (SCUML) registration is mandatory for Designated Non-Financial Businesses and Professions (DNFBPs) in Nigeria. Non-compliance attracts heavy fines and business shutdown.

🛡️
Who Must Register

Is Your Business Required to Register?

⚖️
Legal Practitioners
Lawyers, notaries, and legal firms handling client funds, real estate transactions, or company formations must register with SCUML.
🏠
Real Estate Agents & Developers
All real estate businesses facilitating property transactions above statutory thresholds require SCUML certification.
💎
Dealers in Precious Items
Jewelry dealers, gem traders, art dealers, and dealers in precious metals must maintain SCUML registration.
📊
Accountants & Financial Advisors
Chartered accountants, auditors, tax consultants, and financial advisors operating in Nigeria require SCUML compliance.
Registration Steps

The SCUML Registration Process

We handle the entire registration process so you don't face compliance risks.

🔎

Business Classification Review

We determine whether your business falls under DNFBP categories and which specific SCUML registration class applies to your operations.

📑

AML/CFT Policy Development

We help you develop the mandatory Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Financing of Terrorism policies required for registration.

🖊️

Online Application Filing

We complete and submit your SCUML registration application through the official portal with all supporting documentation attached.

🏦

Fee Payment & Verification

We guide you through the official payment process and coordinate with SCUML for document verification and inspection if required.

🎫

Certificate Issuance

Receive your SCUML Certificate of Registration. We also help you set up ongoing compliance monitoring.

Why It Matters

Consequences of Non-Compliance

⚠
Heavy Financial Fines

Non-registered DNFBPs face fines up to ₦10 million for first-time offences under the Money Laundering Act.

⚠
Business Closure

SCUML has powers to shut down non-compliant businesses through the EFCC.

⚠
Bank Account Restrictions

Non-compliant businesses may have their corporate bank accounts frozen by the Central Bank of Nigeria.

⚠
Prosecution & Imprisonment

Repeat offenders can face criminal prosecution with prison terms of up to 3 years for directors and officers.
